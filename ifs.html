The 5 Whys: Uncovering Perfectionism

09:42

Sha

Simon sinek said ask 5 whys to understand down there; i asked R why he tends to drag things or underestimate delivery (time) eg making delay in wedding -> he said, he wants to have perfect-> but when i asked why again, he said not a why, it's like going 'up' "so that i can take care of you"; can you list 5-10 possible pathways of why going deeper from where he's stuck as possible answers so he can 'hypothesize' and help articulate. Claude

You're right that his shift from "why" (going down) to "so that" (going up to purpose/justification) is a classic deflection when getting close to something tender or unsafe. The perfectionism → caretaking leap skips crucial layers.

Fear-Based Pathways

  • "Because if it's not perfect, something bad will happen / I'll fail you"
    • Deeper: Because I learned that my value depends on getting things right
    • Deeper still: Because when I got things wrong as a child, I lost safety/love/approval
  • "Because I'm afraid of what people will think/judge"
    • Deeper: Because I learned I had to manage others' emotions/perceptions
    • Deeper still: Because someone important made their disappointment my responsibility
  • "Because if I rush and it's not perfect, I'll be exposed as inadequate"
    • Deeper: Because I don't trust that I'm enough as I am
    • Deeper still: Because my worth was conditional on performance

Control-Based Pathways

  • "Because if I control the timing, I control the outcome"
    • Deeper: Because letting go/moving forward feels dangerous
    • Deeper still: Because moving forward in the past led to punishment or abandonment
  • "Because delaying gives me time to make sure I can handle it"
    • Deeper: Because I don't trust myself to handle things in real-time
    • Deeper still: Because I was overwhelmed/alone when I needed support as a child

Enmeshment-Related Pathways

  • "Because I need to make sure everyone else is okay first"
    • Deeper: Because my needs come last / don't matter
    • Deeper still: Because I learned that caring for myself meant someone else suffered
  • "Because if I move forward, I'll lose something/someone important"
    • Deeper: Because commitment to you feels like abandoning someone else (mother?)
    • Deeper still: Because I was made responsible for someone's emotional wellbeing

Worthiness-Based Pathways

  • "Because I don't believe I deserve this yet"
    • Deeper: Because I haven't 'earned' the right to happiness/marriage
    • Deeper still: Because I learned love was transactional, not inherent
  • "Because if we do it now with what we have, it means I'm not successful enough"
    • Deeper: Because my worth is tied to what I can provide materially
    • Deeper still: Because I learned I had to compensate for just being me

Autonomy/Identity Pathways

  • "Because rushing feels like I'm not in control of my own life"
    • Deeper: Because someone else always controlled the timing/decisions
    • Deeper still: Because my autonomy was never respected, so I reclaim it through delay

Questions to Help Him Test These:

  • "When I imagine the wedding happening 'imperfectly' or sooner, what feeling comes up in my body?" (fear? shame? panic? guilt?)
  • "Who taught me that perfect was the requirement?" (parent? circumstance?)
  • "What do I imagine will happen if I 'fail' at this?" (your reaction? his own self-judgment? someone else's response?)
  • "Is there a part of me that feels like I'm betraying someone by moving forward?" (enmeshment red flag)
  • "What age do I feel like when I think about the wedding timeline?" (might reveal the wounded age)

IFS: The Roadmap for Long-Haul Healing

09:46

Sha

Which one would the best approach to try self-therapy on: Internal Family Systems (Schwartz's), or Trauma-Informed therapy, or reparenting inner child wounds? Given we're doing this long-haul for and with each other while only very occasionally will go to professional therapist (given the money, we may go to therapist once in 1-1.5 months only).

Claude

Given your constraints and goals, Internal Family Systems (IFS) is your best primary approach, with elements of inner child reparenting woven in.

IFS is Optimal Because:

  • Self-guided structure - IFS has the clearest methodology for self-therapy without constant professional oversight. The 6 F's (Find, Focus, Flesh out, Feel toward, beFriend, Fear) give you repeatable steps.
  • Handles both your patterns - R's enmeshed "manager" parts (perfectionism, delay) and your "exiled" parts (needing to be be seen wholly) fit perfectly into IFS framework.
  • Mutual witnessing works - You can practice "Self-energy" presence for each other.
  • Non-pathologizing - IFS assumes all parts have positive intent, which reduces shame.

Why Not the Others:

  • Trauma-Informed Therapy - This is more of a lens than a method. It informs how you do therapy but doesn't give you the step-by-step what. IFS is inherently trauma-informed anyway.
  • Pure Inner Child Reparenting - Powerful but can be destabilizing without professional support if you hit deep wounds. It's also less structured for couples work - you might both regress simultaneously with no "adult" in the room.

Your Practical Approach:

Months 1-2: IFS Foundation

  • Both read Self-Therapy by Jay Earley
  • Practice the 6 F's individually first
  • Weekly check-ins: "What parts showed up for you this week?"

Months 3-4: Couple IFS Work

  • Read You Are the One You've Been Waiting For by Richard Schwartz - specifically about using IFS in romantic relationships
  • Practice "U-turns" - when triggered, pause and ask "which part of me is activated? Which part of my partner?"

Months 5-6: Inner Child Integration

  • Add Homecoming for specific inner child exercises
  • By now you understand IFS parts language, so "inner child" work becomes "accessing exiled young parts"
  • Create rituals: weekly "parts check-in dates"

Self-Energy, Qawwam & Masculine Polarity

09:52

And how and if these ensures healthy polarity ad female and male (qawwam) in him, eg i said to him, when he proactively lead wedding (security to me), or give me more (like 4 things when i give 3), then i can see how he's masculine and a leader

Claude

YATOYYBWF = "You Are the One You've Been Waiting For"

Richard Schwartz's book on using IFS specifically in romantic relationships. The title captures the core insight: you can't heal your wounded parts by finding the "perfect partner" - you have to become the internal caregiver (Self-energy) for your own parts first.

Building R's Internal Resources:

  • Daily Self-Parts Check-in (5-10 min, alone) - "Can I (Self) provide that, or is this part asking someone else (Sha) to provide it?"
  • The "6 F's" Solo Practice - WITHOUT you present initially
  • Reparenting Visualization - Weekly practice where R visualizes meeting his younger self
  • "Ask Myself First" Protocol - When R wants to ask you for something emotional, pause and ask internally first

IFS as Qawwam Development:

Self-energy IS the foundation of healthy masculine:

  • Centered (not reactive)
  • Generative (can provide/protect from secure base)
  • Can hold tension (doesn't collapse when you're upset)
  • Moves toward challenge with presence, not avoidance

The Difference:

Externalized (Current):

"Sha, tell me the wedding timeline is okay" (seeking your approval to soothe his anxiety)

Internalized (Goal):

"My anxious part is scared about the timeline. Let me sit with it... Okay, I've checked in with myself."

The Qard Hasan Proposal

Claude

Islamic Framework:

1. Khadijah (RA) and the Prophet (SAW)

Khadijah was wealthy, employed Muhammad (SAW) as her trade agent, and their relationship began with her investing in his capacity. She proposed marriage to him when he had less material wealth.

2. Qard Hasan (Beautiful Loan)

"Who is it that would loan Allah a goodly loan so He may multiply it for him many times over?" (Al-Baqarah 2:245)

3. The Ansari Women Supporting Emigrant Men

Temporary material disparity doesn't negate masculine leadership if the man has Deen, vision, and capacity to grow.

The Discernment Question:

Can you give this with a completely open hand, trusting Allah for the outcome, without ANY expectation of R's behavior changing?

If yes, it's sadaqah/qard. If no, it's control.
